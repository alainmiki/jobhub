Resume Tips

Professional advice to create a standout resume that gets noticed by employers.

Resume Essentials

Key Sections to Include

Contact Information

Name, phone, email, LinkedIn, portfolio (if applicable)

Professional Summary

3-5 sentence overview of your experience and goals

Work Experience

Reverse chronological order, quantifiable achievements

Education

Degrees, certifications, relevant coursework

Optional Sections

Skills

Technical skills, soft skills, languages

Projects

Personal projects, open-source contributions

Certifications

Professional certifications and licenses

Awards & Honors

Recognition, scholarships, achievements

Best Practices

  • Keep it to 1-2 pages for most professionals
  • Use action verbs (achieved, improved, managed)
  • Quantify achievements with numbers and metrics
  • Tailor your resume for each job application
  • Use consistent formatting and professional fonts
  • Proofread multiple times for errors

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't include irrelevant personal information
  • Avoid generic objective statements
  • Don't use tiny fonts or strange formatting
  • Avoid listing duties instead of achievements
  • Don't include references or "references available upon request"
  • Avoid outdated or incorrect contact information

ATS Optimization Tips

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) are used by most companies to filter resumes. Follow these tips to ensure your resume passes through ATS filters:

Keyword Optimization

  • • Include relevant keywords from the job description
  • • Use industry-specific terms and skills
  • • Balance keywords naturally in context
  • • Include both abbreviated and full terms (e.g., "SEO" and "Search Engine Optimization")

Format for ATS

  • • Use standard fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman)
  • • Avoid tables, columns, graphics, or unusual formatting
  • • Save as .docx, .pdf, or plain text
  • • Use clear section headings

Content Structure

  • • Put contact info at the top
  • • Use standard section names (Experience, Education, Skills)
  • • Include quantifiable achievements
  • • Keep consistent date formats
